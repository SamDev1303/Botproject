# Clinical Documentation System

## Purpose
Structured, legally defensible documentation for community mental health support work.

---

## 1️⃣ SHIFT NOTE TEMPLATE

**Tone:** Objective, factual, chronological, risk-aware, legally defensible, professional clinical standard.

**Language:** Third-person, clinical. No slang, no emotional opinions, no "I felt," no dramatics. Only observable behaviour, risk, intervention, outcome.

### Structure

```
SHIFT NOTE – [Client Full Name]

Date:
Shift Time (Allocated / Actual):
Location:
Support Workers on Shift:
Support Type:

---

PRESENTING SITUATION / OVERVIEW
- Client's state (awake/asleep, calm/agitated, withdrawn/verbal)
- Emotional regulation
- Known triggers from handover
- Risk level at start (low/moderate/high)
- Context (recent discharge, sleep deprivation, medication changes, etc.)

Example: "Client awake at commencement of shift. Presentation guarded and irritable. Reported minimal sleep overnight. Risk assessed as moderate due to mood instability."

---

CHRONOLOGICAL SUMMARY OF SHIFT

0900 hrs – 1000 hrs
[Activity, engagement, behavioural changes, medication prompts, emotional shifts, escalation/de-escalation, environmental factors]

1000 hrs – 1100 hrs
[Continue in time blocks...]

Language rule:
- NOT: "She was being dramatic."
- WRITE: "Client raised voice, pacing noted, verbal hostility directed toward staff."

---

RISKS / SAFETY / BEHAVIOUR

Bullet points:
• Verbal aggression
• Self-harm behaviour
• Property damage
• Paranoid ideation
• Absconding risk
• Medication refusal
• Police involvement
• Environmental safety concerns

Risk Rating: LOW / MODERATE / HIGH
Status: Increased / Decreased / Remained Stable

Never exaggerate. Never minimize.

---

SUPPORTS & INTERVENTIONS PROVIDED

Emotional:
• Validation
• Active listening
• Grounding techniques
• De-escalation

Practical:
• Meal prep
• Cleaning
• Appointment support
• Transport
• ADL support

Clinical prompts:
• Medication prompting
• Safety planning discussion
• Psychoeducation

Always show what you did to reduce risk.

---

OUTCOME OF SHIFT

- Client emotional state
- Risk level
- Environment safety
- Who client was left with
- Handover provided

Example: "Client calm at conclusion of shift. No active self-harm behaviour observed. Environment secured. Handover provided to oncoming staff."

---

CONSEQUENCES OF BEHAVIOUR / INCIDENT / MEDICATION ERRORS / UNAUTHORISED RESTRICTIVE PRACTICES

• Police attendance
• Ambulance transport
• ED presentation
• Staff injury
• Property damage
• Service disruption
• Missed appointments
• Behavioural contracts
• Medication errors (if any)
```

## 2️⃣ INCIDENT REPORT TEMPLATE

**Tone:** Extremely factual, emotionless, chronological, no interpretation, court-defensible.

### Structure

```
INCIDENT REPORT – [Client Name]

Date of Incident:
Time of Incident (Start–End):
Location:
People Involved:

---

ANTECEDENTS (MANDATORY)

What occurred before the incident:
• Triggers
• Environmental changes
• Staff changes
• Sleep deprivation
• Medication refusal
• External stressors

No blame language.

---

WHAT HAPPENED (FACTS ONLY)

• Exact behaviour
• Exact statements (quoted if relevant)
• Physical actions
• Escalation pattern

NOT: "She lost control."
WRITE: "Client began shouting, struck wall with open hand, and spat toward staff."

---

ACTIONS TAKEN

Step-by-step:
• Verbal de-escalation
• Removal of hazards
• Police contacted (time)
• Ambulance contacted (time)
• Supervisor notified (time)
• Physical safety repositioning (if used)

---

SERVICES CONTACTED / NOTIFICATIONS

• Police (time, response)
• Ambulance (time, response)
• Supervisor (time, response)
• On-call (time, response)
• Case manager (time, response)
• Family (time, response)

---

OUTCOME

• Where client ended up
• Physical condition
• Emotional presentation
• Risk status
• Staff injury (if any)

---

FOLLOW-UP REQUIRED

• Monitoring
• Clinical review
• Medication review
• Behaviour support update
• Additional staffing

---

CONSEQUENCES OF INCIDENT

• Hospital admission
• Police involvement
• Property damage
• Service escalation
• Restrictive practice review
```

## 3️⃣ VIVA ENGAGE MESSAGE STYLE

**Tone:** Neutral, informative, team-based, brief, clear. No emotional commentary. No long essays.

**Purpose:** Internal communication, daily updates.

### Structure

```
Client Name – Daily Update

[Short paragraph covering:]
• Mood
• Engagement
• Activities
• Medication compliance
• Appointments
• Notable behaviours
• Risk level
```

**Example:**
"Larissa was awake and engaged during shift. Presented tired and reported hunger but initially refused meals. After staff encouragement, client consumed small portion of food. Medication taken as prescribed. Mood improved by mid-shift."

---

## Key Principle

Documentation is:
- Legal armor
- Professional identity
- Evidence of competence
- Protection against false allegations

**The more objective your language, the safer you are.**
