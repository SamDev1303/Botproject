# Clean Up Bros — Facebook + Instagram Growth System (Western Sydney)

## 1) Market Reality (data-backed)

- **Digital reach is not the bottleneck:** Australia has **20.9M social media identities (77.9% of population)** in 2025 (DataReportal).
- **Service area is growing:** Greater Sydney added **107,500 people (+2.0%)** in 2023–24, with strongest growth in outer-west / outer-SW pockets (ABS).
- **Core catchment is large:** Greater Western Sydney has ~**2.5M people**, projected to **3M by 2036** (Western Sydney University).
- **Paid social economics are viable for local services:** Facebook benchmark averages (all industries): **CTR 0.90%**, **CPC $1.86**, **CVR 9.21%**, **CPL $21.98** (WordStream cited by Brafton AU).

### What this means for Clean Up Bros
1. Demand pool is expanding in Western Sydney.
2. FB/IG can be operated profitably if lead quality controls are tight.
3. The growth lever is not “more posting”, it is **offer-message-audience match + fast follow-up**.

---

## 2) Core Growth Model (execution)

### Funnel
1. **Attention:** Reels + before/after + local proof (FB/IG).
2. **Intent capture:** Click to DM / Instant Form / Call Now.
3. **Qualification:** Suburb + service type + timeframe + bedrooms/bathrooms.
4. **Conversion:** Quote in <15 min during business hours.
5. **Retention:** Review request + referral prompt + 30/60-day reactivation ad.

### Offer stack to rotate (not generic “book now” only)
- End-of-lease: “Bond-back checklist clean”
- Fortnightly home clean: “First visit deep reset + ongoing plan”
- Airbnb turnover: “Same-day restock + photo confirmation”
- NDIS/general support clean: “Consistent cleaner + documented scope”

---

## 3) Audience Structure (Meta)

### Geo (priority)
- Liverpool, Fairfield, Blacktown, Parramatta, Penrith, Campbelltown + 10–15km radius clusters.

### Campaign audiences
- **Cold 1 (broad):** 24–55, homeowners/renters, geo only.
- **Cold 2 (service intent):** move/lease/real estate/property-management interests.
- **Warm:** engagers (365d), video viewers (50%+), IG profile engagers.
- **Hot:** website visitors + lead form openers non-submitters (retarget 7/14/30d).

### Budget split (starting point)
- 60% cold prospecting
- 25% warm retargeting
- 15% hot retargeting

## 8) KPI Framework (weekly operating targets)

### Topline
- Leads/week
- Qualified lead rate (% with suburb + service + timeframe)
- Bookings/week
- Cost per booking

### Platform KPIs (Meta)
- CTR (link): target **>=1.0%** (beat benchmark 0.90%)
- CPC: target **<=A$2.20** initially, drive toward **<=A$1.90**
- Lead form completion rate: **>=15%**
- CPL: target band **A$18–A$30** by service line

### Sales execution KPIs
- First response time: **<15 min** business hours
- Quote-to-book rate: **>=30%** warm leads, **>=20%** cold leads
- No-show / ghost rate

## 9) A/B Testing Plan (90-day loop)

### Weekly test cadence
- Run **1 major test/week** (avoid over-fragmenting spend).

### Test sequence
1. **Offer test** (bond-back vs fixed-price package)
2. **Hook angle test** (urgency vs risk-reduction vs convenience)
3. **Creative format test** (UGC talking head vs before/after montage)
4. **CTA test** (DM keyword vs instant form)
5. **Audience test** (broad vs interest stack)

### Decision rules
- Minimum per variant before decision: **1,500 impressions OR 20 clicks OR 5 leads**
- Primary winner metric: **lowest CPL**
- Secondary checks: qualified lead rate + booking rate
- Kill rule: pause variant if CPL is **>35% worse** after threshold reached

## 10) 30-Day Deployment Plan

### Week 1
- Build 12 creatives (3 per pillar priority: transformation/trust/local/objection)
- Launch 2 campaigns: Home Cleaning + End-of-Lease

### Week 3

### Week 4

- Build next-month content from winning hooks + objections
